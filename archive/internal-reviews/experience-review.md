# MegaMind Experience Review

Date: 2026-06-14

## 1. Layout Experiments

Version A remains the existing structure: static pages, split hero sections, modular CSS, and restrained cards.

Version B was explored through larger-feeling hero treatments without changing the page architecture. The hero images now sit in atmospheric containers with subtle grid texture, stronger institutional presence, and page-specific identity classes.

Version C was explored through report and dossier elements: briefing strips, record rows, archive surfaces, and restrained field-note panels.

Implemented low-risk experiments:

- homepage briefing strip and orientation record
- About page map/orientation treatment
- Research page field-note treatment
- Papers page archive/catalog treatment
- EARL page implied operational-risk treatment

## 2. Visual Recommendations

Keep the existing logo and imagery. The images already carry the right history: maps, archives, notebooks, operations rooms, and field evidence.

Best visual direction:

- full brand lockup in navigation
- large documentary hero images
- restrained institutional labels
- report-like section surfaces
- archive/list treatments for papers and research

Avoid:

- decorative technology graphics
- over-explained lore
- novelty labels
- anything that makes EARL feel like a character

## 3. Identity Observations

MegaMind is strongest when it feels like an office that has accumulated hard-won orientation over time.

The best identity signal is not spectacle. It is disciplined recordkeeping under pressure.

The route-through-complexity idea should show up as structure: what happened, what is known, what remains unresolved, and what a serious operator can do next.

## 4. Homepage Alternatives

Current role:
Operational orientation.

Implemented experiment:
Briefing-style status labels and a short orientation record under the hero.

Recommendation:
Keep this direction if it feels quiet and authoritative in Live Server. The homepage should feel like a briefing room, not a sales page.

Useful labels:

- Status
- Orientation
- Active System
- Observed Condition

Use sparingly.

## 5. About Page Alternatives

Current role:
The Map.

Implemented experiment:
Map/orientation hero treatment plus a short "Map notes" panel.

Recommendation:
This page should explain how MegaMind thinks, not merely who MegaMind is. The map framing is strong because it gives the visitor a method before giving them a pitch.

## 6. Research Page Alternatives

Current role:
Field Notes.

Implemented experiment:
Field-note labels, archive surface, and a dossier note about reconstructable evidence.

Recommendation:
Research should feel like a working record. Future content should preserve distinctions between observation, assumption, validation, and unresolved questions.

## 7. Earl Page Alternatives

Current role:
A known operational risk.

Implemented experiment:
EARL is framed as a known condition with active status and an implied risk note.

Recommendation:
Do not define EARL too quickly. Let the page imply recognition first. The visitor should feel that they have encountered the pattern before, even if they do not yet have the name for it.

Avoid mascot energy, joke framing, or over-explanation.

## 8. Elements Worth Testing In Live Server

Test these before finalizing:

- homepage briefing strip readability on mobile
- whether the subtle grid texture feels institutional rather than decorative
- hero image crops at mobile widths, especially `earl-hero.webp`
- paper card left-rule treatment in the archive section
- EARL operational-risk note tone
- whether section density feels established rather than unfinished

Likely keep:

- full-logo navigation
- hero imagery
- archive/report section language
- field-note and dossier structure

Possibly revise after visual review:

- intensity of grid texture
- number of status labels
- exact wording of EARL risk note
- whether the homepage orientation record should remain above or below the three-card section
