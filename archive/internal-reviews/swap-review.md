# MegaMind Homepage/About Image Swap Review

## 1. Homepage Observations

Swapping `about-hero.webp` onto the homepage shifts the first impression from operations toward orientation. The site feels more like a place that begins with a map, a frame, and a method for understanding conditions before action.

This better supports the question of what MegaMind is for: preserving orientation under complexity.

## 2. About Page Observations

Swapping `index-hero.webp` onto About makes the page feel more operational and less explanatory. The About page becomes less "The Map" and more "the office in motion."

That can work, but it makes the About page slightly less distinct from the homepage.

## 3. Which Image Better Communicates MegaMind

The map and compass image better communicates MegaMind as a system of orientation.

The operations image better communicates activity, capability, and operational seriousness.

For core identity, orientation appears stronger than operations. MegaMind is most distinctive when it feels like it helps people understand what happened and where they are, not merely that it operates under pressure.

## 4. Recommendation

Hybrid Alternative.

Use the map and compass image as the homepage hero because it communicates the deeper identity: orientation through complexity.

Use the operations image where the site needs to show applied work, active systems, or program execution. The About page may still benefit from a quieter orientation image long term, but the current experiment suggests the homepage should lead with orientation rather than operations.
