File: The Map Function: Origin

Why this exists

Systems fail slowly, then all at once, then someone asks who has the spreadsheet.

MegaMind exists because complex work does not usually break from lack of effort. It breaks when context, dependencies, and memory are allowed to live in separate rooms.

Operating stance

The hard part is not complexity. The hard part is losing orientation inside it.

Organizations accumulate systems the way cities accumulate roads: one justified connection at a time, followed by decades of people trying to remember why that turn exists.

MegaMind studies the point where the map, the operator, the workflow, and the exception all disagree. That is usually where Earl appears. Not as a person, exactly. More as evidence that the system has been outsourcing memory.

Map notes

Begin with the known terrain.

Keep the frame visible.

The work begins by separating event, context, constraint, and consequence before anyone starts improving a process they have not actually mapped.

Clarity

Say what is known, what is assumed, and what still needs a witness.

Maintainability

Keep the work legible enough that the next person can disagree with it productively.

Deployability

Prefer durable artifacts over systems that require a ceremony to remember how they work.
