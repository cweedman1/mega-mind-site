Classification scale

Internal severity language for notes that keep returning.

  • Level 1: Common KnowledgeEveryone knows it, which is why nobody wrote it down. Example: the side channel is faster than the official channel.
  • Level 2: Known ConditionThe pattern has been observed more than once and is beginning to look structural. Example: the temporary solution has a maintenance calendar.
  • Level 3: Operational HazardThe condition can interrupt work if the wrong person is unavailable. Example: the current process depends on someone remembering the exception list.
  • Level 4: Meeting GeneratorThe problem now creates meetings faster than decisions. Example: ownership is unclear, definitions conflict, and every resolution creates a subgroup.
  • Level 5: Executive Briefing RequiredThe map has reached someone who did not know there was a map. Example: the unofficial workflow is now required for public reporting.
